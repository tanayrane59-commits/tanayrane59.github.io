What is IPO?

IPO stands for Initial Public Offering. It is the process through which a private company becomes a public company by offering its shares to the general public.

Why Do Companies Launch an IPO?

Companies need money to expand their business, develop new products, repay loans, or improve operations. By launching an IPO, companies can raise large amounts of capital from investors.

How Does an IPO Work?

Before an IPO, a company is privately owned. During the IPO process, the company decides the number of shares and their price. Investors apply for these shares, and after allotment, shares are listed on the stock exchange.
