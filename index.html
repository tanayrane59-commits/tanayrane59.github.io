Types of Shares Explained for Beginners

Types of Shares

Shares represent ownership in a company. When you buy shares, you become a part-owner of that company. However, not all shares are the same. There are different types of shares, each with its own features, benefits, and risks.

Equity Shares

Equity shares are the most common type of shares. People who own equity shares are called shareholders. They have voting rights and can participate in company decisions. If the company performs well, equity shareholders benefit the most.

Preference Shares

Preference shares provide fixed dividends and are given priority over equity shares when dividends are distributed. However, preference shareholders usually do not have voting rights.

Bonus Shares

Bonus shares are issued by companies to existing shareholders free of cost. These shares are given instead of cash dividends and increase the total number of shares held.

Rights Shares

Rights shares are offered to existing shareholders at a discounted price. This allows current investors to maintain their ownership percentage in the company.

Conclusion

Understanding the different types of shares helps investors make better decisions. Beginners should start with equity shares of strong companies and gradually learn about other share types.
