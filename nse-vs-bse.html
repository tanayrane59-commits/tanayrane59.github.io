NSE vs BSE

In India, shares are traded on stock exchanges. The two main stock exchanges are the National Stock Exchange (NSE) and the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE).

What is NSE?

NSE is India’s largest stock exchange by trading volume. It is fully electronic and known for high liquidity.

What is BSE?

BSE is Asia’s oldest stock exchange. It has a large number of listed companies and introduced the Sensex index.

Main Differences

  • NSE has higher trading volume
  • BSE has more listed companies
  • NSE index: Nifty 50
  • BSE index: Sensex

Conclusion

Both NSE and BSE are reliable and regulated. For beginners, understanding both helps in learning how Indian markets function.
